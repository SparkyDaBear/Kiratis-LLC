# Vector Asset Specification

## Purpose

This document defines the visual rules for all vector-based site assets so the brand stays consistent across logos, icons, ornaments, dividers, and decorative illustrations.

The intended look is:

- Ancient science.
- Minimalist.
- Artistic, like a living painting.
- Earthy and pastel, never harsh or neon.

Vector assets should feel hand-composed and symbolic, but they must remain clean enough to work at small sizes and inside a modern website layout.

## Core Design Language

### Shape

- Favor circles, arcs, halos, rings, ellipses, and softly irregular geometry.
- Use triangles, diamonds, and polygonal forms sparingly and only when they feel ritualistic or scientific.
- Keep silhouettes simple and legible.
- Avoid sharp aggressive angles unless they are being used as a very small accent.
- Prefer layered shapes that suggest depth through overlap rather than dense detail.

### Geometry

- Build assets from a small number of repeated geometric ideas.
- Use symmetry when the asset is meant to feel emblematic, ceremonial, or symbolic.
- Use gentle asymmetry when the asset should feel painterly or alive.
- Leave negative space inside forms so the design can breathe.
- Use thin orbit lines, measured grids, and subtle radial construction where appropriate.
- Keep linework intentional and controlled rather than decorative for its own sake.

### Texture and Edge Treatment

- Vector work should be clean, but not sterile.
- Use slightly organic curves and softened endpoints instead of perfect machine precision everywhere.
- If texture is needed, keep it implied through shape layering or stroke rhythm rather than heavy effects.
- Do not overuse gradients in vector art. If a gradient is necessary, keep it muted and broad.

## Color Palette

The palette should feel earthy, aged, and luminous.

### Primary Colors

- Parchment: `#E8DDC8`
- Clay: `#C58C6D`
- Sage: `#8DAA91`
- Oxide: `#9C6B4E`
- Slate Blue: `#6E87A3`
- Deep Ink: `#263245`

### Accent Colors

- Faded Gold: `#C9A86A`
- Dust Rose: `#D4A4A0`
- Moss: `#6F8261`
- Pale Aqua: `#B7D7D3`

### Background and Neutral Support

- Warm White: `#F4EFE5`
- Sand: `#D8CBB6`
- Stone: `#A89C8C`
- Charcoal: `#1F232B`

### Color Rules

- Keep saturation low to moderate.
- Prefer muted color relationships over strong contrast.
- Use warm neutrals as the dominant base.
- Reserve deep ink or charcoal for outlines, typography, or anchor marks.
- Use one accent color at a time when possible.
- Avoid bright pure blues, pure reds, or neon greens.
- Never let the palette drift into glossy tech aesthetics.

## Asset Families

### 1. Brand Mark and Logo Elements

Purpose: establish the visual identity.

Shape guidance:

- Use a monogram, seal, sigil, or composed glyph.
- Integrate circles, concentric rings, or a halo-like framing device.
- Keep the mark readable at favicon size.
- Let the form suggest both science and craft.

Geometry guidance:

- Build from 1 to 3 core shapes.
- Favor strong silhouette and restrained detail.
- If using a symbol, keep it centered and balanced.

Color guidance:

- Primary version: Deep Ink on Parchment.
- Alternate version: Parchment on Deep Ink.
- Accent version: Deep Ink with a single Faded Gold or Sage detail.

### 2. Icons

Purpose: support navigation, service labels, and light informational moments.

Shape guidance:

- Use simple line icons or reduced symbol forms.
- Favor icons built from circles, capsules, rays, small grids, and orbital paths.
- Keep each icon visually weight-consistent with the others.

Geometry guidance:

- Use a shared stroke system.
- Keep corners softened unless a specific scientific instrument needs a sharper profile.
- Stay compact and easy to recognize.

Color guidance:

- Use Deep Ink for base icon strokes.
- Use Sage, Slate Blue, or Clay for limited highlights.
- Avoid multicolor icons unless there is a strong reason.

### 3. Hero Illustration

Purpose: create the living-painting first impression for the homepage.

Shape guidance:

- Build a wide composition with layered atmospheric forms.
- Include subtle celestial, botanical, or alchemical references.
- Use floating rings, field lines, diagram fragments, or spectral washes.
- Keep the scene airy and minimal rather than crowded.

Geometry guidance:

- Establish a dominant central form and one or two supporting secondary forms.
- Use asymmetrical balance.
- Let some forms fade or dissolve at the edges.
- Keep the composition calm even if it feels expressive.

Color guidance:

- Base the piece in Warm White, Sand, Parchment, and Stone.
- Add Sage, Dust Rose, Pale Aqua, or Faded Gold as soft atmospheric accents.
- Use Deep Ink sparingly to anchor focal points.

### 4. Section Illustrations

Purpose: support consulting, junk removal, and computer build pages with thematic visuals.

Shape guidance:

- Consulting: abstract molecular lattices, field diagrams, data flow arcs, or layered geometric study forms.
- Junk removal: simplified hauling silhouettes, bins, cleanout stacks, or practical transport motifs.
- Computer builds: elegant component outlines, chassis profiles, thermal lines, or structured panel forms.

Geometry guidance:

- Match the illustration geometry to the service content.
- Keep consulting art more abstract and analytical.
- Keep junk removal art more direct and physical.
- Keep computer build art precise and modular.

Color guidance:

- Consulting: Slate Blue, Sage, Pale Aqua, Deep Ink.
- Junk removal: Clay, Oxide, Sand, Stone.
- Computer builds: Deep Ink, Slate Blue, Faded Gold, Warm White.

### 5. Ornaments and Dividers

Purpose: provide subtle structure without visual clutter.

Shape guidance:

- Use glyphs, ticks, rings, nodes, dots, short rays, or delicate border fragments.
- Think of them as margin notes from an illuminated manuscript or scientific plate.
- Keep ornamentation sparse.

Geometry guidance:

- Use repeated rhythm rather than heavy pattern density.
- Ensure the dividers are consistent with the rest of the system.
- A divider should feel like a quiet framing device, not a dominant illustration.

Color guidance:

- Usually Deep Ink, Stone, or Sage.
- If needed, add a single muted accent such as Faded Gold.

### 6. Textures and Background Motifs

Purpose: give the site its living-painting atmosphere.

Shape guidance:

- Use paper grain, wash edges, soft mineral clouds, and atmospheric stains.
- Favor broad irregular fields over small repetitive noise.
- Keep these assets abstract.

Geometry guidance:

- Background motifs should not compete with content.
- Use them as ambient fields, corner blooms, or subtle full-bleed layers.

Color guidance:

- Warm White, Parchment, Sand, Stone, and very soft Sage or Dust Rose.

## Minimum Asset Set

If only a first pass is being created, produce these assets first:

1. Primary logo mark.
2. Secondary horizontal wordmark.
3. Favicon or simple symbol.
4. One homepage hero illustration.
5. One consulting section illustration.
6. One junk removal section illustration.
7. One computer build section illustration.
8. Two or three decorative dividers or ornaments.
9. One paper or grain texture.
10. One atmospheric background wash.

## Illustrator Export Guidance

- Keep the master file in `.ai` format.
- Export logos, icons, and dividers as `.svg`.
- Export painterly hero art and backgrounds as `.webp`.
- Export transparent overlays as `.png` only when needed.
- Use descriptive filenames with hyphens.

Examples:

- `logo-primary.svg`
- `logo-mark.svg`
- `hero-astral-field.webp`
- `divider-ring.svg`
- `texture-parchment.webp`

## Quality Checks

Before finalizing an asset, verify that:

- It is legible at the intended size.
- It feels quiet and composed, not busy.
- It matches the earthy pastel palette.
- It supports the site without overpowering the text.
- It still feels like Kiratis LLC rather than a generic fantasy or science illustration.
